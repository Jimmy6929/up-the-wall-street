# 06 — Monitor & Sell (ch. 14, 17)

Buying isn't the end of the work — it's the start of monitoring. Re-examine each holding's **story** on a cadence, and sell for **fundamental** reasons only. Used by `/recheck`.

## Rechecking the story (ch. 14)
Re-tell the [two-minute drill](04-story.md) roughly every quarter and around earnings. Ask the three core questions:
1. **Still cheap vs. growth?** Has the PEG / P/E-vs-growth gotten stretched? (re-run the numbers)
2. **Still growing from here?** What is happening to make it grow *going forward* — not just what already happened?
3. **Any new obstacles?** New competition, debt, saturation, a stalled catalyst?

Track **category-specific signals:** fast grower — is the runway shrinking / expansion saturating? cyclical — where in the cycle, what are inventories doing? turnaround — is the comeback materializing and debt under control? asset play — is the hidden value being recognized?

> The proven-but-not-yet-saturated middle innings of a fast grower are often the **best** time to add.

## Good times to buy (ch. 17)
Not market timing — opportunism when fear does the pricing:
- **Year-end** tax-loss selling and window dressing push good companies down.
- **Market crashes / sharp declines** drag excellent companies down with the bad indiscriminately.
Buy good companies (especially Watchlist names at your pre-set level) when fear marks them down.

## When to sell — by category
Sell because the **story has played out or broken**, judged by category:
- **Fast grower:** growth is clearly ending — expansion slowing, runway gone, P/E ballooned far above the growth rate.
- **Stalwart:** risen substantially and the P/E is stretched, or rotate into a cheaper equivalent.
- **Cyclical:** near the **peak** — inventories rising, margins about to roll over (often when the P/E looks deceptively *low*).
- **Turnaround:** it has fully recovered and is now priced as a healthy company (no longer a turnaround).
- **Asset play:** the hidden value has been recognized and unlocked.

## Never sell for the wrong reasons
- ❌ Because the **price dropped** (panic), ❌ because it **rose** (nerves), ❌ because **nothing's happened yet** (boredom).
- ❌ Don't set an arbitrary "I'll sell when it gets back to even" target — the stock doesn't know your cost.
- ✅ **Watering the weeds is the cardinal error:** selling winners to bank a gain while holding losers to break even is exactly backwards. Let winners run while the story is intact; sell losers when the story is broken.

## Output of `/recheck`
- The re-told story and the three answers (cheap? growing? obstacles?).
- Updated numbers (re-run the scripts).
- **Decision: Hold / Add / Sell**, with a category-specific, fundamentals-based reason — never citing price/fear/boredom.
- Update `watchlist.md` / `portfolio.md` accordingly.
