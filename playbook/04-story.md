# 04 — The Two-Minute Drill / The Story (ch. 11)

Before any verdict, you must be able to tell the stock's **story** in about two minutes of plain language: **what the company does, why it will grow (the catalyst), and what could go wrong.** If you can't tell it clearly, you don't understand it well enough to recommend it. A good story can be told to a spouse or a child without finance jargon.

The story is also your **monitoring script** — you'll re-tell it in [06 — Monitor](06-monitor-and-sell.md) to decide hold/add/sell.

## What the story must cover, by category

- **Fast grower:** *How and where will it keep growing? How long is the runway? Is growth accelerating or slowing? Is the expansion already profitable and proven in a few markets?*
- **Stalwart:** *Is the P/E reasonable? Has it run up too far? Has anything stalled the steady growth? Any diworseification?*
- **Cyclical:** *Where are we in the cycle? What are inventories and capacity doing? Are earnings near a peak or a trough?*
- **Turnaround:** *Is the recovery actually happening? Can the balance sheet (debt) survive the raid by creditors?*
- **Asset play:** *What are the assets worth? Are there hidden ones? Who or what unlocks the value, and when?*
- **Slow grower:** *Is the dividend safe and well-covered? (Usually this is as far as it goes.)*

## The plain-language test
Write the drill as 3 short beats:
1. **What it is** — one sentence a non-investor understands.
2. **Why it grows** — the specific catalyst (more stores, repeat purchases, a niche, a recovery, a hidden asset), tied to earnings.
3. **What breaks it** — the honest risks (the seed of the bear case).

If you stumble, hedge, or can't name what would break the thesis, that's the gap in the research — fill it before the verdict.

## Output of this step
- A **two-minute drill** (the 3 beats above), tailored to the category.
- A one-line **headline story** for the watchlist (e.g., "membership flywheel; quality at a full price").
- An explicit list of **what would break it** (handed to the skeptic in [the bear case] and reused as sell triggers).
