# 05 — The Final Checklist → Verdict (ch. 15)

Verify before you recommend, using a **category-specific** checklist. The P/E-vs-growth question is universal; everything else depends on the category. Run every candidate through the same structured questions appropriate to its type — this is the discipline that prevents buying on story alone.

## Universal checks (every stock)
- **P/E vs. growth (PEG):** Is the P/E high or low for this company and its peers? Is it near the growth rate? (script-computed)
- **Story:** Is there a clear, followable two-minute drill?
- **Institutional ownership:** The *lower* the better — room to be discovered.
- **Insider activity & buybacks:** Insiders buying? Company repurchasing shares?
- **Earnings record:** Consistent and growing?
- **Balance sheet:** How much cash, how much debt? ("No debt can't go bankrupt.")

## Category-specific emphasis
- **Stalwart:** the key issue is **price** — is the P/E reasonable? Watch for over-extension after a big run, stalled growth, and diworseifications.
- **Fast grower:** Is there a **long runway** left, and is the expansion **profitable and already proven** in a few markets? Is the P/E near the growth rate? Be suspicious of **50–100% growth rates** (unsustainable).
- **Cyclical:** Watch inventories and supply/demand; know where you are in the cycle; **anticipate the P/E shrinking as earnings peak — a low P/E here is a danger sign, not a bargain.**
- **Turnaround:** Can it survive its debt? Is the recovery actually underway? Big money is made when a troubled company genuinely turns.
- **Asset play:** What are the assets worth? Are there hidden ones? How much debt detracts? Will the value be unlocked, and by what catalyst?

## Mapping the checklist to a verdict

Output exactly one verdict, justified only by fundamentals (never price action or excitement):

- **Buy candidate** — category is favorable; PEG attractive (or, for non-PEG categories, the value/recovery is real and verifiable); balance sheet sound; story clear with a long-enough runway; green flags outweigh red; the bear case is survivable. *(Fast grower with PEG < 1 + proven runway + net cash is the archetype.)*
- **Watchlist** — good business, wrong price or wrong moment. Common for a **stalwart** whose P/E is rich (PEG > 1), or a fast grower you'd want cheaper. Worth owning when fear marks it down (ch. 17). Record the price/level that would change the verdict.
- **Pass / Avoid** — red flags dominate (hottest-in-hottest, "the next X", whisper stock, single-customer), or a **cyclical at peak earnings** (low P/E trap), or no constructable story / no earnings to value, or a balance sheet that can't survive. Say plainly why.

> [!warning] Hard stops that force Pass/Avoid regardless of other metrics
> - Cyclical clearly at/near a peak (record earnings, near-peak capacity, rising inventories) → **not** a buy on a low P/E.
> - "The next [famous winner]" framing or hottest-stock-in-hottest-industry with 90%+ institutional ownership and insider selling.
> - No earnings and no revenue with the case resting on a single binary/miracle outcome (whisper stock).

## Output of this step
- The filled **checklist** (universal + category-specific).
- **Verdict:** Buy candidate / Watchlist / Pass/Avoid.
- **One-paragraph justification** in fundamentals terms, plus the **bear case** and (if Buy/Watch) the **sell triggers set in advance**.
