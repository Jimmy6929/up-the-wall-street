# 01 — Classify the Stock (ch. 07)

**Classify before you analyze.** The category sets what return to expect, how much risk you're taking, what to check, and when to sell. The *same* 40% gain means "hold, it's just starting" for a fast grower and "take it and leave" for a stalwart. Do this step first; everything downstream depends on it.

## Two preliminaries

- **Effect on the whole company.** A hot product only matters if it's big enough to move *total* earnings. Retin-A barely moved giant J&J; L'eggs made tiny Hanes. Always ask: what does this success do to the bottom line, as a % of sales/profits?
- **Big companies make small moves.** A multibillion-dollar giant can't grow tenfold ("GE would have to take over the world"). For big upside, look to smaller companies; for safety and modest gains, the giants. Match expectations to size.

## The six categories

| Category | Profile | Earnings growth | What to expect | Strategy |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Slow grower** | Large, mature, aging | ~2–4% (≈ GNP) | Low upside, generous dividend | Mostly avoid; own for income only |
| **Stalwart** | Big, established, high-quality | ~10–12% | Steady gains, downside protection; rarely a tenbagger | Buy at a fair price; take ~30–50% and rotate; ballast |
| **Fast grower** | Small, aggressive | ~20–25%+ | The superstocks — where tenbaggers live; highest risk | Hold while growth + story continue; watch the runway |
| **Cyclical** | Sales/profits swing with the economy (autos, airlines, steel, chemicals) | Rises and falls | Timing is everything; often mistaken for a stalwart at the wrong moment | Buy near the cycle bottom, sell near the top; **a low P/E at peak earnings is a danger sign** |
| **Asset play** | Sitting on something valuable Wall Street overlooks (cash, real estate, a subsidiary) | n/a — value is in assets | Hidden value waiting to be unlocked | Buy the gap; local/insider edge helps most; wait for the catalyst |
| **Turnaround** | Beaten-down, troubled, near-bankrupt | n/a — recovery-driven | High reward if the recovery is real | Confirm recovery is underway and the balance sheet can survive |

## How to classify

1. **Size & maturity:** How big is it? Mature giants → slow grower or stalwart. Small and growing → fast grower.
2. **Earnings growth rate:** ~2–4% slow; ~10–12% stalwart; ~20%+ fast grower. (Use the script-computed historical rate plus a forward estimate.)
3. **Economic sensitivity:** Do sales/profits track the business cycle? → cyclical (do NOT read its low P/E at peak as cheap).
4. **Hidden value:** Is the market price below cash/real-estate/parts value? → asset play.
5. **Distress + recovery:** Troubled but mending, survival hinges on debt? → turnaround.

If hype dominates and fundamentals are thin (no/again-negative earnings, "the next X", a miracle story), it is not really any of these — treat it via [02 — Screen](02-screen.md) as a stock to **avoid**, not a category to force-fit.

> [!tip] Companies migrate
> A fast grower matures into a stalwart, then a slow grower; a stumbling cyclical becomes a turnaround; a grower with lots of real estate becomes an asset play. Lynch notes Disney has been in nearly every category. Re-classify as the business changes (see [06 — Monitor](06-monitor-and-sell.md)).

## Output of this step
- **Category** (one of the six, or "avoid — hype over fundamentals").
- **One-sentence reason** grounded in size + growth + economic sensitivity + hidden value/distress.
- **Expectation set** (e.g., "stalwart → steady, not a tenbagger" / "fast grower → tenbagger potential, high risk").
