# RKLB — Rocket Lab Corp

> [!summary] Verdict: **Pass / Avoid**
> A real, fast-growing space business — but with **no earnings to value**, trading at **~100x sales** (market cap ~$63–66B on ~$602M revenue), where the marginal value rests on an **unflown rocket (Neutron)** and a defense-constellation buildout. Negative EPS every year + "the next SpaceX" framing + heavy insider selling is exactly the screen Lynch says points to **Avoid regardless of the metrics**. The 18-analyst average price target ($103.91) is *below* today's price — even the bulls model no upside. Not a fundamentals-justified buy.

## 1. Lead (ch. 06)
The idea came in as a bare ticker request (`/research RKLB`) — a name that's been one of the most talked-about stocks of the 2025–26 space rally. **A lead is not a buy signal** (ch. 06). Noticing a thrilling, widely-touted story is a reason to *investigate*, not to act — and "exciting and widely-touted" is itself a yellow flag (gate 4). What follows is the homework.

## 2. Classification (ch. 07)
**Category:** **Fast grower — but unprofitable**, and on the screen it migrates toward **"avoid: hype over fundamentals."** Rocket Lab is a small-cap-grown-large ($63–66B) vertically-integrated launch + space-systems company whose **revenue** grows fast (+38% FY2025, +63.5% in Q1-2026) but whose **earnings are negative every year 2022–2025**. Per playbook/01, when "no/again-negative earnings" combine with a "the next X" / miracle-growth story, it "is not really any of these [six categories] — treat it via the screen as a stock to **avoid**, not a category to force-fit."

**Expectation set:** A fast grower is where tenbaggers live *and the highest risk lives* — but the discipline (ch. 15) is to be suspicious of **≥50% growth rates** (unsustainable) and to demand a **P/E near the growth rate**. Here there is no P/E and no positive earnings, so the usual fast-grower math (PEG) cannot anchor a verdict. The expectation must therefore be set by survival economics and the price already paid — both of which are unfavorable.

## 3. Screen (ch. 08–09)
- **Green flags:** A few real ones. **Niche / vertical integration** — Electron is the #2 US orbital launch vehicle by cadence; Space Systems is now the majority of revenue and backlog. **Institutional ownership only ~50%** (below Lynch's >85% "crowded" bar — room left to be discovered). **Strong balance sheet** (~$1.07B net cash, little real debt). It is a genuine, growing operating business — not a pure shell.
- **Red flags (dominant):**
  - **No earnings.** Negative EPS every year 2022–2025; consensus EPS stays negative through 2026. There is nothing to value on (ch. 13).
  - **Hottest stock in the hottest industry.** Space is *the* glamour sector of this cycle; glamour bids the price past any earnings support and draws fierce competition (ch. 09, the red flag to avoid "above all").
  - **"The next SpaceX."** The thesis is openly sold this way — almost the canonical "the next [famous winner]" tell, which "often marks the peak."
  - **Heavy insider selling into the run-up.** CEO Beck sold >$140M (Dec 2025); CFO Spice >$100M (Jan 2026), plus more in 2026. Largely 10b5-1, but the scale is large and one-directional into a ~400% move.
  - **Relentless dilution.** Diluted shares +14.2% YoY; Q1-2026 alone added ~23M shares (convert conversions) + ~6.4M (ATM), raising ~$450M. Growth is partly bought with dilution.
  - **Government-customer concentration.** ~74% of the $1.85B backlog is Space Systems, with >$1.3B tied to one agency (Space Development Agency) — appropriations/shutdown risk in a single customer.
- **Excitement check: FAILS.** The bull case leans heavily on narrative — "the space economy," "Flatellite/constellation ambitions," "the next SpaceX," a reusable rocket yet to fly. When the thesis rests on thrill rather than earnings, Lynch says slow down and re-examine the earnings — and the earnings are negative.
- **Provisional lean: AVOID.** Per playbook/02, "no earnings + hottest-in-hottest + 'the next X' + insider selling … strongly points to Avoid, regardless of a superficially okay" metric.

## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** Rocket Lab builds and launches small rockets (Electron, the #2 US orbital launcher by number of flights) and increasingly builds satellites and spacecraft parts for others (the "Space Systems" business, now the bigger half). It is trying to graduate to a larger, reusable rocket called **Neutron** to compete in the medium-lift market that SpaceX dominates.
2. **Why it *could* grow:** Revenue is compounding fast (+38% to +63% YoY) as Space Systems wins large government/defense constellation contracts (>$1.3B from the Space Development Agency), and if **Neutron flies and ramps**, Rocket Lab could capture launch revenue at much higher dollar-per-mission than Electron. Losses are narrowing quarter over quarter.
3. **What breaks it (the honest risks):** **There are no profits, and the price already assumes a near-perfect future.** Neutron has *never reached orbit* and has slipped 2024 → 2025 → early 2026 → no-earlier-than Q4 2026 after a tank-test failure — the medium-lift thesis rests on a vehicle that doesn't yet exist. The company **burns ~$320M/yr and funds it with dilution** (+14% shares/yr). Revenue is **concentrated in one government agency** subject to appropriations. And launch is a brutal-margin business against SpaceX (165 launches in 2025 vs ~20 Electron). If you can't tell the story without "the space economy" and "the next SpaceX," the excitement *is* the thesis — gate 4.

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
**Strongest refutation:** At ~$63–66B on $601.8M revenue (~100x+ sales, ~104x EV/Sales) with **negative earnings every year** and consensus EPS still negative through 2026, the entire valuation is a discounted bet that **Neutron — a rocket that has never reached orbit and has slipped repeatedly, most recently after a Jan-2026 propellant-tank failure — plus a defense-constellation buildout will execute flawlessly for years.** This is the textbook Lynch trap: the hottest stock in the hottest industry, sold as "the next SpaceX," where the hope is already in the price — confirmed by an 18-analyst average target ($103.91) that sits *below* the current price ($110.08). With no earnings to anchor, it is closer to a story/whisper stock than an analyzable fast grower, and it fails the "know what you own" valuation test.

**Concrete disconfirming facts:**
- ~100x+ sales, zero profits; one model flags it ~96% overvalued; analyst consensus target is *below* the price (no modeled upside).
- **Neutron has slipped 3+ times and still has zero orbital flights**; latest target Q4-2026 after a stage-1 tank test failure.
- **Insider selling dwarfs the first-pass figure:** CEO Beck >$140M (Dec 2025), CFO Spice >$100M (Jan 2026) + more in 2026 — into a ~400% run-up.
- **Dilution:** diluted shares +14.2% YoY; Q1-2026 added ~23M (converts) + ~6.4M (ATM), ~$450M raised. Per-share value grows far slower than revenue.
- **Burn:** FY2025 FCF ≈ −$321.8M (~$232M of TTM burn on Neutron); runway shrinks if Neutron slips again.
- **Concentration:** >$1.3B of a $1.85B backlog tied to a single agency (SDA); 10-K flags government-funding disruption as a material risk.
- **Competition:** SpaceX did 165 launches in 2025 vs RKLB's ~20 Electron; Neutron enters a market Falcon 9 already wins on price/reuse.

**What would have to be true for the bull case to survive:** (1) Neutron reaches orbit near the Q4-2026 target and ramps without another failure; (2) revenue compounds ~40–60%/yr for *years* **and** margins inflect to real GAAP profit; (3) dilution decelerates (requires FCF to turn positive); (4) the SDA/defense backlog converts on schedule with no appropriations disruption. Each is outside the comfort zone of a disciplined, fundamentals-first buyer, and several are outside the company's control.

## 7. Final checklist (ch. 15)
**Universal:**
- **P/E vs. growth (PEG):** ❌ n/m — negative earnings; ~100x sales. No earnings-based anchor exists.
- **Story:** ⚠️ Tellable, but the load-bearing parts (Neutron, constellation) are *future/unproven*; leans on excitement.
- **Institutional ownership:** ✅ ~50% — the one genuinely favorable box (room to be discovered).
- **Insider activity / buybacks:** ❌ Heavy net insider *selling*; no buyback (issuing shares instead).
- **Earnings record:** ❌ Negative and consistent — every year 2022–2025.
- **Balance sheet:** ✅ Net cash ~$1.07B, ~3.7yr+ runway — strong, but a survival metric, not a thesis.

**Category-specific (fast grower):**
- **Long runway?** ⚠️ Arguably yes for the space TAM — but unproven and unpriced-for.
- **Expansion profitable & already proven in a few markets?** ❌ Not profitable anywhere on a net basis; the growth engine (Neutron) is *pre-revenue*.
- **P/E near the growth rate?** ❌ No P/E; growth is revenue, not earnings.
- **Suspicious of ≥50% growth?** ❌ Yes — the 51.3% forward estimate trips the unsustainable-growth flag.

**Hard stop check:** Not a cyclical-at-peak. But it hits the screen's Avoid combination — **no earnings + hottest-in-hottest + "the next X" + heavy insider selling** — which playbook/02 says points to Avoid regardless of the metrics.

## 8. Verdict & sell triggers
**Verdict: Pass / Avoid** — justified only by fundamentals: there are **no earnings to value**, the price is **~100x sales** with the marginal value resting on an **unflown rocket**, the screen's dominant red flags (negative earnings, hottest-in-hottest, "the next SpaceX," heavy insider selling, +14%/yr dilution) outweigh the real green flags (net cash, ~50% institutional ownership), and the **analyst consensus target is below the price**. This is *not* a comment on Rocket Lab as a company or a forecast that the stock will fall — it's that a disciplined, fundamentals-first buyer has nothing to underwrite a purchase *here*. Per gate 5, the honest call is that at this price, with the thesis resting on Neutron + constellation hopes, this is outside what the method can recommend buying.

**What would move it onto the buy track (set in advance, so excitement can't move the goalposts):**
- **Neutron reaches orbit and demonstrably ramps** (recurring medium-lift launch revenue), *and*
- **Free cash flow inflects toward positive** (burn stops, dilution decelerates), *and*
- A price that makes the **revenue multiple defensible** once earnings exist (a real P/E that can be put next to the growth rate).

Until then it stays a **watch-only** name — tracked, not owned. (If a human already holds it, this note is a Hold-with-eyes-open at most, not an add; the sell discipline would be: exit if Neutron fails its debut or slips materially again, or if the cash runway compresses below ~2 years without a financing path.)
