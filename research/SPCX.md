## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** a holding company of four stories — the world's dominant rocket launcher, the Starlink satellite ISP, the xAI compute/AI lab, and X (Twitter) — controlled by one person.
2. **Why it would grow (the claim):** Starlink subscriptions (10.3M, +134% YoY) keep compounding; Starship V3 slashes deployment cost; the AI buildout converts into contracted compute revenue (~$26B/yr signed).
3. **What breaks it:** Starlink ARPU has already collapsed $99 → $66 while segment operating income went flat ($1.03B → $1.19B Q1→Q1) — growth is being bought with price cuts as Amazon Leo arrives; the compute contracts carry 90-day outs from customers who compete with Grok; Starship is behind plan (5 of 25 targeted flights flown in 2025); AI capex (~$30B/yr run-rate) consumes the cash pile in ~2–3 years if contracts lapse.

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
The skeptic was sent to attack *both* directions and could not break the Avoid — but broke two of its grounds, and this note carries the corrections. **The steelman that failed on price, not business:** Starlink is genuinely excellent, the AI segment has $26B/yr of contracted tenants, and the balance sheet survives any attack ($61B net cash) — yet the most credible published valuation of that disciplined bull case is Morningstar's **~$780B DCF** ("overvalued by roughly half" *at the IPO target*, before the first-day pop); at ~$2.16T the market pays ~2.8× the steelman. **The bear that survives:** the one Lynch-able asset is deteriorating in exactly the dimension the multiple needs — subscriber growth +134% bought with 15–40% price cuts and free hardware, margin compression not expansion; the compute "diversification" is two customers with 90-day outs renting GPUs that Grok's reportedly falling usage freed up; Starship is load-bearing for everything (including 1.3B Mars-milestone shares) and behind plan every year so far; and insiders marked at $1.25T in the February tender (CNBC IPO live coverage, 2026-06-12) can begin selling at a ~73% markup (script-computed: $2.16T / $1.25T) from 2026-08-20. Buying at this price requires a conjunction of ~50–70% probability events to all land at ~100%.
