# BRBR — BellRing Brands, Inc.

> [!summary] Verdict: **Pass / Avoid**
> A former fast grower whose earnings have *reversed*: TTM EPS ($1.29) is below FY2024 ($1.86) and FY2025 ($1.68), management cut FY2026 EBITDA guidance **twice in one quarter**, and gross margin fell ~1,180 bps. The 6.8× P/E and 0.55 PEG are a **value trap** built on a stale, pre-cut growth estimate — not a bargain. Net debt (−$1.15B) exceeds the entire market cap against a 6.0× covenant. The repeat-purchase staple thesis just cracked. Not a buy on a low P/E; revisit only if margins, demand, and guidance stabilize.

## 1. Lead (ch. 06)
Surfaced by the `/scan` run on 2026-06-07 (ranked #11 by PEG; flagged for repeat-purchase + spinoff + buyback green flags). **A lead is not a buy signal** — and this is the case study for why: the screen's attractive-looking PEG rested on a backward-looking growth rate that the homework shows has reversed.

## 2. Classification (ch. 07)
**Category:** **Fast grower whose growth has reversed.** BellRing is a mid-cap (~$1.0B market cap after an ~86% decline) maker of Premier Protein ready-to-drink shakes + Dymatize powders, spun off from Post Holdings (2022). It grew diluted EPS from $0.70 (FY2021) to $1.86 (FY2024) — a genuine fast grower — but EPS then *fell* to $1.68 (FY2025) and $1.29 TTM. It is **migrating toward turnaround territory**, but the recovery is **not underway** (the business is still deteriorating), so it does not qualify as a turnaround buy either.
**Expectation set:** a fast grower is bought for a long, proven, *profitable* runway with PEG near the growth rate. That premise has failed — growth is negative and the runway premise (household repeat-purchase) just took its first crack in five years. Expect continued earnings risk, not compounding.
**Effect on the whole company:** Premier Protein ≈ the company (single brand) and the club channel is ~40% of sales — so the brand's stumble *is* the company's stumble. Concentration cuts both ways and is now cutting down.

## 3. Screen (ch. 08–09)
- **Green flags (now hollowed out):** repeat-purchase staple (#10) — *but household buy-rate just declined for the first time in 5 years*; clean spinoff from Post (#4); "aggressive buybacks" (#13) — **reclassify as a red flag** (capital destruction, see below); leading niche RTD-protein brand.
- **Red flags:** earnings **declining two straight years**; FY2026 EBITDA guidance **cut twice in one quarter** ($425–440M → $315–335M); gross margin −~1,180 bps in a quarter; **leveraged** (net debt −$1.15B > market cap; 6.0× covenant ceiling with leverage rising as EBITDA falls); **single-brand + single-channel concentration**; reliance on third-party co-manufacturers; **94.97% institutional ownership** (crowded — opposite of a Lynch setup); CEO retirement announced on bad news; buybacks executed at ~$29 (FY26) and ~$40–72 (FY25) vs ~$9 today.
- **Excitement check:** the only thing "exciting" is the *low multiple itself* — i.e., the cheapness is the lure (excitement-by-spreadsheet). The protein/GLP-1 tailwind narrative has inverted into a promotional war. The thesis cannot rest on thrill; the earnings say avoid.

## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** BellRing makes Premier Protein ready-to-drink shakes (and Dymatize powders) — convenient, repeat-purchase protein nutrition sold heavily through club stores (Costco/Sam's).
2. **Why it *was* supposed to grow:** more households buying protein more often (a secular tailwind), distribution/category expansion, and a leading brand — compounding into ~20%+ EPS growth, amplified by buybacks.
3. **What breaks it — and just did:** a commodity/tariff cost shock (whey inflation) crushed gross margin; ~40 new RTD-protein entrants (Fairlife, Huel) triggered a promotional war (price/mix −9%, ~27% of category volume on promotion); and households cut their buy-rate for the first time in five years (value-driven trade-down). Margin is squeezed from both sides at once with no clean lever, on a leveraged single-brand balance sheet.

**Headline story (for the watchlist):** *"Protein-shake fast-grower whose growth reversed — twice-cut guidance, margin war, leveraged; a low-P/E value trap, not a bargain."*

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
The skeptic's independent investigation (confirmed the price is real — no stock split / data artifact; 116.3M sh × $8.77 ≈ $1.02B cap, P/E ~6.8 ties out):

1. **The "E" is still falling, so the P/E is fiction.** EPS already $1.86 → $1.29; with Q2 FY26 EBITDA down 55% and full-year guidance cut twice to ~$325M midpoint, forward EPS heads lower. 6.8× on a number declining 30%+ is a classic value trap.
2. **Margins are broken on both sides at once.** Gross margin −~1,180 bps from a cost shock *and* a pricing war it cannot pass through (~40 insurgents promoting ~60% of the time). Raise price → lose volume; hold price → lose margin. No clean lever.
3. **Leverage is the time bomb.** ~$1.185B debt vs $32.6M cash; net debt larger than the entire market cap; 6.0× covenant ceiling with leverage *rising* as EBITDA falls. A leveraged single-brand name in a price war is the balance sheet to avoid.
4. **The buybacks destroyed capital.** Shares repurchased at ~$40–72 (FY25) and ~$29 (FY26) now worth ~$9 — the "green flag" is evidence of poor capital allocation, funded against a levered balance sheet.
5. **Concentration + crowding + a lame-duck CEO.** Single brand, ~40% club channel (where trade-down bites hardest), 94.97% institutional ownership (no marginal buyer left, plenty left to sell), CEO retiring on bad news.

**The single thing the note must not get wrong:** do **not** present 6.8× P/E / 0.55 PEG as "cheap." They are computed on an earnings/growth base management has already cut twice; the real forward multiple is far higher. Anchor on forward earnings power and leverage-against-falling-EBITDA, not the trailing multiple.

## 7. Final checklist (ch. 15)
**Universal:**
- **P/E vs. growth:** 6.8× looks low but the "E" is *declining* and the PEG's "G" is stale/pre-cut → not cheap; a value trap. ❌
- **Story:** clear and followable — but it's a *breaking* story, not a growth story. ❌ (for a buy)
- **Institutional ownership:** 94.97% — crowded, no room to be discovered. ❌
- **Insider/buybacks:** no insider buying; buybacks executed far above today's price = capital destruction. ❌
- **Earnings record:** grew FY21→24, then *declined* FY25 and TTM; guidance cut twice. ❌
- **Balance sheet:** net debt −$1.15B (> market cap); 6.0× covenant, leverage rising. ❌
**Fast-grower-specific:** runway must be long, proven, and *profitable* — instead growth has reversed, margins collapsed, and the repeat-purchase premise cracked. **Fails.** (Were it judged as a turnaround: recovery is **not** underway and the leveraged balance sheet's survival depends on EBITDA not falling further — also fails until stabilization is evidenced.)

## 8. Verdict & sell triggers
**Verdict: Pass / Avoid** — justified purely on fundamentals: a fast grower whose earnings have reversed (EPS $1.86 → $1.68 → $1.29), with FY2026 EBITDA guidance cut twice in one quarter, gross margin −~1,180 bps, net debt exceeding the market cap against a 6.0× covenant, a cracked repeat-purchase premise, and 94.97% (crowded) ownership. The low P/E and "attractive" PEG are a **value trap** on a deteriorating earnings base — Lynch's textbook hard stop (a low P/E on falling earnings is a danger sign, not a bargain). Buybacks are value-destructive. **Not a buy.**

**Re-evaluate toward Watchlist only if** (the turnaround evidence that does not yet exist):
- Two consecutive quarters of **stabilizing/growing adjusted EBITDA and gross margin** (margin war and commodity costs abating);
- Guidance **stops being cut** (or is raised);
- Evidence the **household buy-rate decline reversed** (the staple premise re-confirmed);
- **Net leverage falling** with comfortable covenant headroom;
- A new CEO with a credible, *evidenced* plan.

Until then: Pass/Avoid. Do not anchor on the low absolute price — wait for the fundamentals to turn.
