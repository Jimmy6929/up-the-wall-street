# CSV — Carriage Services, Inc.

> [!summary] Verdict: **Watchlist**
> A genuinely defensive, non-cyclical death-care niche (Lynch's "something depressing about it" archetype) — but **not a buy at this price**. PEG is **1.83 (rich)** on ~7% real forward growth, the balance sheet is **leveraged ~4×** (net debt ≈ the entire equity value; ~1 turn of covenant headroom; $397.5M notes due 2029), organic funeral volume is **declining** (−5.8% comparable contracts), a structural **cremation** shift erodes revenue/margin per case, and a **$100M ATM** is diluting shareholders. The scan's "cheap PEG 0.72" was an artifact of a one-off-inflated historical CAGR. Worth owning only at a materially lower price and/or after it delevers and organic volume re-inflects.

## 2. Classification (ch. 07)
**Category:** **Stalwart** — the #2 publicly-traded US death-care company (funeral homes + cemeteries), a small-cap (~$0.6B) growing via acquisitions + pricing + pre-need sales. Demand is **non-cyclical / recession-resistant** (demographically driven) — explicitly **not** a cyclical, so no peak-P/E cyclical trap applies. It has small-cap, moderate-grower, roll-up characteristics rather than blue-chip-stalwart quality.
**Expectation set:** stalwart → steady, defensive, *not a tenbagger*; the key questions are **price (is the PEG reasonable?)**, **balance-sheet leverage**, and whether the steady growth has stalled. **Effect on whole company:** acquisitions and same-store pricing move total earnings; the secular shift to (cheaper) cremation is the structural drag, and organic case volume is currently shrinking.

- **Excitement check:** **low — which is green** ("boredom is often green"). The thesis correctly rests on defensive earnings, not thrill. The problem is not excitement; it is **price + leverage + a secular headwind**.

## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** Carriage Services owns and runs funeral homes and cemeteries across the US (the #2 public death-care company), serving families through trusted local funeral-home brands.
2. **Why it grows:** steady, recession-resistant demand + price increases + acquisitions of independent funeral homes + pre-need (prepaid) sales that build a deferred backlog. *Caveat: growth is increasingly acquisition- and price-driven — organic case volume is currently falling.*
3. **What breaks it:** the secular shift to **cheaper cremation** (lower revenue + margin per case) that the local-brand niche can't offset; **high leverage** (~4×, ~1 turn of covenant room, 2029 notes to refinance); **ATM dilution**; overpaying for acquisitions; and simply **paying a rich price** (PEG 1.83) for ~7% growth.

**Headline story (watchlist):** *"Defensive death-care roll-up; real niche but fully-priced (PEG ~1.8) and leveraged (~4×), with a structural cremation headwind — watch for a cheaper price."*

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
1. **The "demographic tailwind" isn't showing up — organic volume is shrinking.** Comparable funeral contracts fell **−5.8% YoY** (Q1-26); the headline count looks flat only because acquisitions papered over the decline. Management blamed "compressed death rates" (post-COVID normalization) and a tough flu-season comp. Hope, not a thesis.
2. **The cremation shift is a permanent revenue/margin headwind.** US cremation hit **63.4% (2025), projected 82.3% by 2045**; cremation revenue/case and margins are well below burial. CSV is already ~60% cremation. The sticky local brand picks *which home*, not the cheaper disposition — so the business runs an acquisition escalator just to stand still.
3. **The 15.8% EPS CAGR is an accounting artifact.** 2024 GAAP was depressed by ~$16.9M severance/strategic-review costs; 2025 was boosted by divestiture gains. Clean adjusted path: **$2.65 (2024) → $3.20 (2025)**; FY2026 guidance adj. EPS **$3.35–$3.55 (only +5–11%)**, and Q1-26 adj. EPS was **down 10.4% YoY**. Real organic growth is mid-single digits, ~7% — exactly the forward estimate.
4. **Leveraged small-cap, not a fortress.** Net debt **$519M ≈ the $596M equity value**; ~4.0× vs a 5.0× covenant (≈1 turn of headroom), measured on *adjusted* EBITDA that strips out recurring "special charges"; $397.5M notes due 2029; interest ~$28.4M on ~$135M EBITDA. A soft volume year (which the −5.8% is signaling) plus a 2029 refinancing into higher rates is a real risk for a company this size.
5. **The ATM and the failed 2023 sale say the price is full, not cheap.** A board issuing equity equal to ~⅙ of the market cap doesn't think shares are cheap; one that did would buy back. And in 2023 a strategic buyer (Park Lawn) bid **~$34/share all-cash and walked**, with the board rejecting all proposals — public investors are now paying ~10% above a price an informed, synergy-rich acquirer wouldn't consummate.

**The single thing the note must not get wrong:** do **not** anchor on the 15.8% historical CAGR or the $3.25 GAAP EPS — both are corrupted by one-offs. The honest inputs are normalized EPS ~$3.20 and ~5–8% forward growth, on which PEG is rich (~1.6–1.8). The skeptic leans **Pass**; I land on a *cautious Watchlist* (below) because the underlying industry is genuinely defensive and the company is covenant-compliant, not in distress.

## 8. Verdict & sell triggers
**Verdict: Watchlist** — justified on fundamentals: a genuinely defensive, non-cyclical death-care niche, but **richly priced** (PEG 1.83, rich even on adjusted EPS) for **~7% real growth**, on a **leveraged balance sheet** (~4×, net debt ≈ equity value, ~1 turn of covenant headroom, 2029 notes), with **declining organic funeral volume**, a **structural cremation headwind**, and **ATM dilution**. Good-enough business, wrong price — the classic Watchlist case. The scan's apparent cheapness was an artifact of a one-off-inflated CAGR. (The skeptic leaned Pass; this is a *low-conviction* Watchlist, not a "buy the dip" name.)

**Move to Buy candidate only if** (all, ideally):
- **Price falls enough that PEG approaches ~1.0** on the *real* ~7% growth (a materially lower entry);
- **Comparable funeral volume re-inflects positive** (organic growth, not just acquisitions);
- **Net leverage falls toward management's ~3.5× target without leaning on the ATM**;
- **Adjusted ≈ GAAP earnings** (the one-offs stop).

**Drop to Pass if** leverage rises toward the covenant, the ATM is used heavily to plug the balance sheet, or organic volume keeps declining while cremation mix climbs. Until a trigger fires: watch, don't buy.
