# BA — The Boeing Company

> [!summary] Verdict: **Watchlist**
> A real, directionally-improving turnaround — Boeing returned to GAAP profit in FY2025, deliveries and backlog are at records, and cash burn is narrowing — but it is priced for **flawless** execution: ~$218B / **~87x** a thin, tax-aided first recovery year, capitalizing a **2028** ~$10B free-cash-flow target that doesn't exist yet, on top of **net debt of −$24.7B**. The recovery's *direction* is sound; the *price, timeline, and leverage* leave no margin of safety. Good business, wrong price/moment → **Watchlist with hard triggers**, not a buy here.

## 1. Lead (ch. 06)
Came in as a bare ticker (`/research BA`) — one of the most-watched "the worst is behind it" recovery stories in the market. **A lead is not a buy signal** (ch. 06); a recovery narrative is a reason to do the homework, and "the worst is behind it, this time it's fixed" is itself the kind of exciting story Lynch's gate 4 flags as a *yellow* light. The investigation follows.

## 2. Classification (ch. 07)
**Category:** **Turnaround**, with heavy **cyclical** character. Boeing is a beaten-down aerospace + defense giant emerging from a multi-year crisis (737 MAX groundings, the Jan-2024 door-plug blowout, FAA production caps, **four straight loss years 2021–2024**) that returned to profit in FY2025. Per playbook/01, the turnaround lens governs: the questions are *is the recovery actually, durably underway* and *can the balance sheet survive its debt* — **not** PEG. The cyclical overlay (commercial-aircraft demand tracks the air-travel/economic cycle) means we also watch cycle position, not just the income statement.

**Expectation set:** A turnaround pays off big *only if the recovery is real and you bought the gap before it closed* (ch. 15). The reward is recovery-driven, not growth-multiple-driven. Critically for a giant this size: **big companies make small moves** — a $218B company recovering to ~$10B of FCF is a re-rating story, not a tenbagger. And the script fired **`cyclical_caveat`**: judge by cycle position, capacity, and inventories — PEG is misleading here.

## 3. Screen (ch. 08–09)
- **Green flags:** Genuine ones. **A defensible niche / duopoly** — Boeing and Airbus are the entire large-commercial-jet market; that moat is real and durable. **A deep, durable demand signal** — record **$695B backlog (>6,100 jets)**. **A user, not just a maker, of a recovery** — deliveries jumped to 600 in FY2025 and the 737 rate was approved up to **47/month**, so the operating recovery is visibly underway. **Not in imminent distress** — $29.4B liquidity and equity back to positive.
- **Red flags:**
  - **Brutal leverage (the turnaround crux).** **Net debt −$24.7B** ($29.4B cash & securities vs $54.1B gross debt, swollen by the Spirit AeroSystems re-acquisition). **Annual interest (~$2.8B) exceeds full-year net income ($2.235B).** Moody's (**Baa3, one notch above junk**) expects FCF won't cover the >$12B of debt due over two years — so Boeing is expected to **borrow more, not de-lever.** This is the opposite of a Lynch net-cash cushion.
  - **The FY2025 profit is thin and tax-aided.** Pre-tax income $2.635B; net $2.235B *after a $397M income-tax benefit* — ~2.5% net margin, part of it non-operational.
  - **Still burning cash.** FY2025 FCF −$1.877B; Q1-2026 FCF −$1.5B. The +$1–3B FY2026 guide is contradicted by the Q1 start.
  - **~1,700 backlog jets can't be delivered yet.** MAX 7 and MAX 10 remain **uncertified**, slipped into 2026 over an engine anti-ice / inlet-overheating safety flaw; first deliveries not before 2027.
  - **Defense (BDS) still bleeding** on fixed-price programs (KC-46, T-7, VC-25B) — fresh charges most quarters.
  - **Massive recent dilution.** The Oct/Nov-2024 **$24.25B** raise (largest follow-on in US history; ~129.4M shares + $5.75B mandatory convertible) means per-share recovery is divided across a structurally larger share count.
  - **Insider net selling ~4.4:1 TTM** into the recovery narrative; **~73% institutional ownership** (crowded — little neglected-stock cushion); **dividend suspended since 2020** (no income to wait on).
- **Excitement check: CAUTION.** The bull case leans on the *thrill* of "duopoly + record backlog + inflection." The durable parts (moat, backlog) are real, but the thesis's load-bearing assumptions — FCF inflection, two certifications, defense stabilizing, de-leveraging — are all *future*. Gate 4 says slow down and re-examine the cash earnings; the cash earnings are still negative.
- **Provisional lean: mixed → good business, full price.** No single hard-stop red flag dominates (it's not "the next X," not a whisper stock, not a cyclical at a *low* P/E on peak earnings), but the leverage + price combination is heavy.

## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** Boeing makes the world's large commercial jets (737 MAX, 787, 777) — one of only two companies on earth that can — plus defense systems and services. It nearly broke after the MAX crashes, a 2024 door-plug blowout, and four straight loss years, and is now climbing back.
2. **Why it *is* recovering:** Deliveries jumped to 600 in 2025, the cash-cow 737 line is approved back up to 47/month, the order book is a **record ~$695B (>6,100 jets)** that airlines can't get elsewhere, the company returned to a (thin) GAAP profit, and quarterly cash burn is shrinking. As rates rise and the two delayed MAX variants get certified, each delivered jet converts backlog into cash. The recovery's *direction* is genuinely sound.
3. **What breaks it (the honest risks):** **The price already assumes the recovery finishes flawlessly, and the balance sheet is heavily levered.** Net debt is −$24.7B with interest costs above net income; Moody's expects Boeing to borrow *more*, not pay down. FCF is still negative. ~1,700 backlog jets hinge on MAX 7/10 certifications that have a safety flaw and keep slipping. Defense keeps booking fixed-price losses. One more crash, strike, quality escape, or FAA freeze resets the model to year zero — as 2018, 2019, and 2024 each did. And a record backlog booked at possibly late-cycle airline optimism can convert to *deferrals*, not deliveries, in a downturn.

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
**Strongest refutation:** At $218B / ~87× its first thin recovery-year earnings, the market is already paying for the *destination* — analyst/company FCF target of **~$10B by 2028**, which $250–275 bull targets explicitly capitalize at 20–22.5× — while Boeing is still at the *starting line*, **burning −$1.5B of FCF in Q1-2026** and reporting a profit that only existed because of a **$397M income-tax benefit**. You are not buying a turnaround at turnaround prices; you are pre-paying full price for three more consecutive years of flawless execution (rate ramps, two uncertified-jet certifications, defense stopping its bleed, and de-leveraging) in an industry where Boeing has missed nearly every self-imposed deadline since 2019. If any one leg slips, there is no valuation cushion underneath — the duopoly and $695B backlog are real but already fully in the price.

**Concrete disconfirming facts:**
- **Interest (~$2.8B/yr) > net income ($2.235B).** Debt service consumes the whole recovery.
- **Moody's (Baa3) expects FCF won't cover >$12B of debt due over two years → Boeing borrows more, not de-levers.** The Lynch turnaround crux ("can it survive its debt") is answered "yes, but only by adding debt."
- **FY2025 profit is tax-aided** ($397M benefit) and ~2.5% margin — thin and partly non-operational.
- **2026 FCF guide (+$1–3B) contradicted by the actual −$1.5B Q1-2026 print** — a hope, not a result.
- **~1,700 MAX 7/10 backlog jets still uncertified**, slipped over an engine anti-ice/inlet safety flaw; first deliveries not before 2027.
- **Defense still bleeding** on fixed-price programs (~$5B of losses in 2024; fresh KC-46 charges since).
- **$24.25B dilution in Oct-2024** (largest US follow-on ever) divides per-share recovery across far more shares.
- **Insider net selling ~4.4:1**, ~73% institutional ownership, 0% dividend — the screen's emotional/ownership tells.

**What would have to be true for the recovery thesis to survive (falsifiable):** (1) **Full-year 2026 FCF lands positive** inside the +$1–3B guide despite the −$1.5B Q1 start; (2) **MAX 7 and MAX 10 are both certified on the 2026 FAA timeline** with no new safety findings; (3) **737 holds at 47/month for 18+ months** with no new quality escape, strike, or FAA freeze; (4) **net debt actually falls without further dilution** (repaid from cash, not refinanced); (5) **defense fixed-price losses stop**; (6) **no new crash/grounding** — the single event that resets everything.

## 7. Final checklist (ch. 15)
**Universal:**
- **P/E vs. growth (PEG):** ⚠️ P/E ~87 on a thin recovery year; PEG n/a. Rich, but normal for early-cycle — read by cycle position, not PEG.
- **Story:** ✅ Clear, followable two-minute drill (duopoly + backlog + delivery ramp vs leverage + execution risk).
- **Institutional ownership:** ❌ ~73% — crowded; little discovery cushion.
- **Insider activity / buybacks:** ❌ Net insider selling; no buyback (issued $24.25B of equity in 2024).
- **Earnings record:** ❌ Four loss years then one thin, tax-aided profit — not yet consistent.
- **Balance sheet:** ❌ Net debt −$24.7B; interest > net income; Baa3; more borrowing expected. Survivable, but the opposite of a cushion.

**Category-specific (turnaround + cyclical):**
- **Can it survive its debt?** ⚠️ Yes for now (it has $29.4B liquidity and equity is back to positive) — *but only by refinancing/borrowing more*, not by de-levering. The crux is answered weakly.
- **Is the recovery actually underway?** ✅ Yes, directionally — deliveries 600, 737 to 47/mo, burn narrowing, record backlog, returned to profit.
- **Cyclical: where in the cycle / is the P/E a trap?** ⚠️ High P/E on early-recovery earnings (not the low-P/E-at-peak trap), but a record backlog may embed late-cycle airline optimism that converts to deferrals in a downturn.

## 8. Verdict & sell triggers
**Verdict: Watchlist** — justified only by fundamentals. This is a genuine, directionally-real turnaround in a durable duopoly with a record backlog — there *is* a clear, followable story and a (leveraged but) survivable balance sheet, so it is **not** a Pass/Avoid in the way a no-earnings hype stock is. But it is **not a buy at this price**: ~$218B / ~87× a thin, tax-aided recovery year, with **net debt −$24.7B**, interest above net income, FCF still negative, ~1,700 backlog jets uncertified, and the valuation already capitalizing a **2028** cash-flow target. That is *good business, wrong price/moment* — the textbook Watchlist case. The disciplined posture is to track it and let the recovery prove itself (and ideally let fear mark it down, ch. 17), rather than pre-pay for flawless execution.

**What would move it to Buy candidate (set in advance, so the recovery narrative can't move the goalposts):**
- **Durably positive FCF** — full-year 2026 inside the +$1–3B guide (not one seasonal quarter), *and*
- **MAX 7 and MAX 10 certified** on the 2026 FAA timeline with no new safety findings, *and*
- **Gross/net debt actually falling** without further equity dilution, *and*
- A **price that doesn't already capitalize 2028** (a valuation that leaves a margin of safety on demonstrated, taxed operating cash earnings).

**Sell triggers (if a position is ever taken):** a new crash/grounding or major quality escape; the 737 rate frozen or cut again by the FAA; FY-FCF turning back negative after inflecting; a credit downgrade into junk or a fresh large equity raise; or MAX 7/10 certification slipping into 2027+.
