# PLMR — Palomar Holdings

> [!summary] Verdict: **Watchlist**
> A genuinely good specialty insurer whose headline numbers (PEG 0.79, combined ratio 76.9%) were earned in a zero-catastrophe year at a hard-market peak — the growth is real but its *quality* is shifting from the moaty earthquake franchise (+3% in Q1 2026) to thin-margin crop and unproven casualty. At ~3.1x book with 93% institutional ownership, wait for a cat-tested quarter or a much better price.

## 2. Classification (ch. 07)
**Category:** Fast grower — small (~$3.0B market cap), EPS compounding 42%/yr FY2021→25, forward consensus ~20%. **With a cyclical overlay:** specialty-insurance pricing is itself a cycle, and the screen flagged the whole E&S sector at a hard-market peak.
**Expectation set:** fast grower — the runway and the *durability* of growth are everything; pay up only when both are proven.

## 3. Screen (ch. 08–09)
- **Green flags:** dull, disagreeable niches (earthquake, crop, surety, Hawaii hurricane); repeat-purchase premiums; founder-CEO still operating; new $200M buyback (2026-05-06); residential quake book ~97% retention — a real moat.
- **Red flags:** institutional ownership ~92.9% (crowded — the opposite of undiscovered); repeated routine insider selling, zero open-market buys in 2026; street framing as "the next Kinsale" (a "next X" pattern); sector-wide E&S rate softening.
- **Excitement check:** the thesis isn't thrill-driven, but beware *spreadsheet excitement* — a 0.79 PEG that quietly depends on the two lowest-quality growth lines.

## 5. The two-minute drill (ch. 11)
1. **What it is:** a specialty insurer that writes the risks big carriers avoid — California earthquake above all — and reinsures most of the tail away.
2. **Why it grows:** it keeps opening adjacent dull niches (crop, surety, fronting→builders' risk) while the residential quake book renews at ~97%; FY2025: GWP +31.5%, adjusted ROE 23.6%.
3. **What breaks it:** a major quake or Hawaii hurricane that tests the $3.5B reinsurance tower (guidance budgets only $8–12M of cat losses against a $20M per-event retention); reinsurance repricing after the event; the soft E&S cycle compressing the rates that built the record.

## 6. Bear case (the skeptic, ch. 09/20)
The skeptic's strongest case: **the growth being priced is not the growth being delivered.** The earthquake engine grew just 3% in Q1 2026 with commercial quake renewal rates down ~18%; the headline GWP growth (+42% in Q1 2026, per the Q1 call) now comes from crop (+82%, structurally thin-margin federal MPCI), casualty (+55%, with 85%+ of reserves unproven IBNR) and acquired surety. The 76.9% combined ratio was earned in 2025 — a year with **zero US hurricane landfalls** and no significant quake; the book hasn't been tested by its own peril since Northridge 1994. Fronting — once a named growth pillar — shrank 34% in 2025 on a partner termination. At ~3.1x book, you're paying a compounder multiple for cycle-peak, untested-tail earnings, with 93% of the register already convinced. Mitigants the skeptic conceded: the balance sheet survives the attack, residential quake retention is a real moat, and the new buyback is a genuine green flag.

## 7. Final checklist (ch. 15)
- Universal: P/E vs growth ✓ (0.79 on conservative estimate) · story ✓ but quality-shifting · ownership ✗ (92.9% crowded) · insiders ✗ (selling, no buys) · earnings record ✓ (uninterrupted growth FY21–25) · balance sheet ✓ (D/E 0.31).
- Fast grower specific: runway — partly ✓ (niche expansion works) but the *proven* runway (quake) is decelerating and the new runway (crop/casualty) is unproven; growth durability through a cat event — untested.

## 8. Verdict & sell triggers
**Verdict:** **Watchlist** — justified by fundamentals: a quality, well-capitalized niche underwriter whose current earnings are flattered by a zero-cat year and a hard market, whose core franchise growth has decelerated to ~3%, and whose register is fully discovered at ~3.1x book. Not a Pass — the moat and balance sheet are real.
**Upgrade triggers (evidence, set in advance):** two quarters of quake-segment GWP growth >10%; a cat-loaded quarter where the combined ratio stays <90% (tower proven); favorable development on the 2024–25 casualty accident years; price nearer ~2x book; any open-market insider buy.
**Sell-the-watch triggers:** adverse casualty development; reinsurance costs repricing faster than primary rates; crop loss ratios blowing out in a drought year.
